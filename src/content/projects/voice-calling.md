During this time within the company, many were fed up with creating products to only grease vanity metrics. Teams like mine on Photos began to push for sweeping core changes that focused more on natural user behavior, instead of engagement based features. This began with fixing our sharing experience.

From the beginning, there were three main issues we wanted to address. Firstly, nobody edited photos with filters (that’s what Instagram is for). So editing capabilities were put behind a long press. Second, most took a photo using their device and then opened the app to share it. So a quick access tray was added in the post composer. Third, people despised the fact that photos, once posted, were sorted using an algorithm, rather than respecting their order. So we added clear numbers to the selection to reinforce their choice.

I had the role of product designer on this project. Collaborating with a product manager and several engineers. Together we defined the goals, outcomes, and research required to identify behavioral traits. Followed leading by all aspects of visual design, prototyping, and animation.
