Feed Tagging while a simple project to increase tags on photos became a learning exercise on how to properly educate product usage.

After running experiments with the product lead of Tagging, we came to realize several nuances in the photo-sharing experience. First was that those who knew about tagging rarely did it when posting. Rather after the fact. Second, once posted, most people would then go to their News Feed to see their post in context. Third, a large majority of people didn't even know photo tagging existed!

With these results, we instrumented a small design change to the post experience based on more natural user behaviors. By adding a simple icon and number to identify untagged faces. This was just enough additional education to help people, unfamiliar with the feature, to drive up successful tags on photos by over 5%.

Typical responsibilities on this project included leading the design of both the small experiments and final implementation while collaborating with the product manager to devise user research strategies.
